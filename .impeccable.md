# Digital Sorcery — Design Context

This file is the source of truth for design decisions. The `/impeccable` and `/design-taste-frontend` skills read it before generating any UI. Update it when the brief changes.

---

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary buyers (decision-makers, sign the contract):**
CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Platform Engineering, Director of Cloud Infrastructure, Head of SRE, Head of DevOps. In smaller technical companies: COO or technical Founder.

**Influencers (champion the vendor internally):**
Senior Platform Engineers, Staff Engineers, DevOps leads, Architects, Data/AI platform leads.

**Company size sweet spot:** 100–5,000 employees — large enough to have real engineering pain and budget, small enough to move faster than mega-enterprise procurement.

**Geography:** Denmark / Nordics, Germany, Netherlands, UK, selectively Spain.

**Context of use:**
- **Desktop/laptop = primary.** Buyers research during the workday, in internal evaluation cycles, comparing vendors after a referral or LinkedIn touchpoint. Design desktop-first for credibility.
- **Mobile = secondary.** Phone traffic comes from LinkedIn clicks, late-night curiosity, conference follow-up, founder browsing. Optimize for clarity, not amputation.

**The job they're hiring us for:**

Within 5 seconds, the visitor must feel:
1. *"These people understand modern infrastructure deeply."*
2. *"This is not generic consulting."*
3. *"They can solve expensive engineering problems."*

Before they hit "Book a Call," they must believe:
- **Capability** — knows platform engineering, cloud systems, AI ops, automation.
- **Credibility** — has done serious engineering before, not just decks.
- **Relevance** — understands *my* environment, not theory.
- **Outcome** — can reduce toil, increase reliability, accelerate delivery.
- **Safety** — talking to them is worth my time.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** **Precise · Quiet · Arcane**

- **Precise** — engineered, not vague. Every claim grounded. Every spacing intentional.
- **Quiet** — no chest-beating, no hype, no neon. Controlled confidence. The interface earns trust by *not* trying.
- **Arcane** — advanced knowledge that few truly understand. Not fantasy; specialist depth made legible.

**Alternate framing:** *Clinical · Elegant · Dangerous* — where "dangerous" means powerful capability.

**One-line verdict:** *Digital Sorcery should feel like the consultancy serious engineers hire quietly — not the flashy AI brand everyone forgets.*

**Voice:** Concrete verbs. No buzzwords. Never "elevate," "seamless," "unleash," "next-gen." No "AI-powered" stuffing. Say what we did, what changed, and what it cost.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark only. Deep obsidian (`#050508`). Derived from audience context — engineering leaders evaluating during long workdays and late-night research. Dark is the correct default for this product, not a stylistic reflex.

**References (borrow specific things, not the whole site):**
- **Vercel** — clean hierarchy, sharp typography, product confidence, generous whitespace.
- **Anthropic** — restrained intelligence, calm confidence, *not* the noisy AI aesthetic.
- **Palantir** — serious enterprise gravity, mission-critical feeling, systems trust.
- **Venice.ai** — distinctive type and atmospheric restraint.

**What to specifically borrow:**
- **Type** — large, confident, declarative headlines. Never decorative.
- **Pacing** — fast comprehension up top, slow sophistication on scroll.
- **Density** — substance, not brochure-thin. Real artifacts (architecture diagrams, code, system graphs) over stock illustrations.
- **Motion** — subtle and meaningful. Never attention-seeking.

**Anti-references (disaster if we look like):**
- Web3 / crypto sites — neon gradients, fake futurism, low trust.
- Cheap agency templates — stock handshakes, blob backgrounds, meaningless claims, Fiverr energy.
- "AI startup" template-core — purple-to-blue gradients, glowing CTAs, oversized H1 screaming "AI-powered."

**Typography decision:**
The current Space Grotesk + Inter + JetBrains Mono stack is on the impeccable banned list as training-data defaults. **Recommended swap:**
- **Display:** **Sora** — geometric, calm, technical without being tropey. Free Google Font.
- **Body:** **Manrope** — neutral, readable, less default-feeling than Inter, broad weight range.
- **Mono:** **JetBrains Mono** — keep. Fits engineering audience honestly; not used as decoration.

Pair = display sans + body sans + technical mono. Quiet, precise, no serifs (serifs would push us toward editorial/luxury, wrong register for platform engineers).

**Color palette discipline:**
- 60% deep obsidian surface (`#050508` / `#0c0c10`).
- 30% crystalline silver text + soft borders (`#c8d0e0`, tinted neutrals).
- 10% warm parchment accent (`#E3CCB1`) — used rarely, for labels/verdicts/status only. Rarity is what gives it power.
- **Banned:** purple-to-blue gradients, neon glows, gradient text fills, pure `#000`/`#fff`. The current `--color-ds-violet` and `--color-ds-indigo` tokens should be retired or hidden behind the `mono` theme.

**Visual language:**
- Prismatic glass and crystalline shimmer remain *the* differentiator — but used **sparingly**, on hero artifacts and one or two anchor cards, not on every container. Overuse turns it into glassmorphism slop.
- Replace any `border-left: Npx` accent stripes (currently `.accent-border-card`) — that's an absolute ban. Use full borders, leading numerals, or no indicator at all.
- Asymmetric layouts and generous whitespace > symmetric three-card grids.
- Real engineering artifacts (graphs, diagrams, system maps) > generic illustrations.

### Design Principles

1. **Trust through restraint.** Every removed element raises perceived credibility. If a flourish doesn't earn its place, cut it. The Anthropic/Vercel calm beats the AI-startup shimmer.

2. **Engineered, not decorated.** Spacing, type, and color all derive from the Fibonacci/φ system. Decisions are explicable. Decoration that can't justify itself gets removed.

3. **Density with breath.** Show enough substance to prove depth (artifacts, system thinking, real metrics) — but pace it with deliberate whitespace so leaders can scan in 5 seconds and engineers can dive in 5 minutes.

4. **Quiet motion only.** One orchestrated reveal per section. No perpetual loops competing for attention. Always honor `prefers-reduced-motion`.

5. **Accessibility is credibility.** WCAG 2.2 AA minimum. Visible focus rings. Body text passes contrast on obsidian. Meaning never carried by color alone (gold vs. indigo) — always paired with label, icon, or shape. Mobile body type stays comfortable (≥16px).

---

### Hard Constraints (cross-skill enforcement)

These come from the impeccable + design-taste-frontend skills and override defaults:
- **No** Inter, Space Grotesk, IBM Plex Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, Outfit, Fraunces, Instrument Serif (training-data defaults).
- **No** gradient text (`background-clip: text` over a gradient).
- **No** colored side-stripe borders (`border-left/right` greater than 1px) on cards, list items, callouts, alerts.
- **No** purple/blue AI gradients, neon glows, default `box-shadow` glows.
- **No** generic 3-equal-card feature rows. Use zig-zag, asymmetric grids, or bento.
- **No** pure `#000` or `#fff`. Tint everything.
- **No** emojis in UI. Lucide icons only (already standard).
- **No** "Acme/Nexus/SmartFlow" placeholder names. Use realistic, contextual names.
- **No** `h-screen` for hero. Use `min-h-[100dvh]` to avoid iOS Safari jump.
- **Always** `prefers-reduced-motion` honored on shimmer, orbits, R3F scenes.
