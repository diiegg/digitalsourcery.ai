## 2. Three-Act Narrative

### Act I: Understanding the Challenge (Mirror the Buyer's World)

**For VP of Engineering (Champion):**
"Your platform team is drowning. Every new AI initiative means another bespoke deployment pipeline, another monitoring blind spot, another compliance question your infra team can't answer. Your data scientists ship notebooks; your SREs ship Kubernetes; nobody ships AI products. You're hiring faster but shipping slower. Your CEO wants to know why competitors are shipping AI features and you're still in 'research mode.'"

**For CTO (Economic Buyer):**
"You've invested in ML talent, cloud infrastructure, and executive promises. But production AI systems are fragile, expensive, and opaque. Your board wants to see ROI. Your auditors want to see controls. Your customers want to see features. Right now, you're burning budget on experiments that never ship, vendor relationships that lock you in, and consultants who deliver PowerPoints instead of pipelines."

**The Tension:**
Moving fast breaks compliance. Moving carefully breaks velocity. Your current approach optimizes for neither. The hyperscalers sell you building blocks but no blueprint. The Big 4 sell you strategy but no systems. Your team is stuck between tools that don't integrate and timelines that don't move.

---

### Act II: The Solution Journey (How DS Resolves the Tension)

**The DS Approach:**
We don't build AI systems. We build **AI-native platform engineering** — the layer between hyperscaler services and your messy reality.

**What that means:**
1. **Discovery Sprint (Week 1-2):** We map your current state — not with workshops and whiteboards, but by reading your Terraform, interviewing your on-call engineers, and stress-testing your assumptions. We identify the 3 highest-impact platform capabilities that unlock AI velocity.

2. **Production Implementation (Week 3-7):** We ship working infrastructure using 80% existing tools (OpenAI/Anthropic APIs, Langchain, Postgres/Pinecone, Datadog/Grafana). We write the 20% custom code that makes them coherent. Golden Path templates. Model serving pipelines. Cost attribution dashboards. Compliance audit trails. All in infrastructure-as-code you can extend.

3. **Transition to Product Ownership (Week 8):** We don't hand you a 200-page runbook. We pair with your team on live incidents. We document decisions, not configurations. We train your platform engineers to own the system, not depend on us.

4. **Retained Strategic Guidance (Post-Engagement):** Your platform evolves. New AI vendors emerge. Your auditors ask new questions. We stay on retainer as your Fractional CAIO — monthly roadmap reviews, on-call for architectural decisions, quarterly executive briefings.

**Why This Works:**
- Faster than Big 4 (we ship code, not decks)
- More coherent than freelancers (we design systems, not scripts)
- More aligned than hyperscaler services (we have no cloud margin to protect)
- More sustainable than full-time hires (you get judgment without overhead)

## 5. Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "You're too small"
**What they mean:** "If this fails, I can't blame McKinsey. If I hire you and it fails, I'm fired."

**Response:**
"You're right — we're not McKinsey. Which means you get me, not a rotating cast of junior consultants executing a pre-sold methodology. You're hiring specialized expertise in AI-native platform engineering, not a brand name.

Here's the risk calculus: Big 4 costs $500K and takes 6-12 months to deliver a strategy deck. We cost $80-100K and deliver working infrastructure in 8 weeks. If we fail, you've spent 1/5th the budget and lost 1/6th the time. If we succeed, you're shipping AI features while your competitors are still in 'discovery workshops.'

Plus, our retainer model means skin in the game. If the infrastructure doesn't work, you stop paying. McKinsey gets paid whether you ship or not.

Want to talk to a fintech CTO who took the same risk?"

---

### Objection 2: "We'll build it internally"
**What they mean:** "Why pay you when we can learn by doing?"

**Response:**
"You absolutely could build this internally. The question is: should your platform team spend 6-12 months learning AI infrastructure patterns, or should they spend that time enabling your product roadmap?

Here's what internal build looks like:
- 3-6 months researching vector databases, LLM observability, cost attribution
- Another 3-6 months building, breaking, rebuilding
- Opportunity cost: features your platform team didn't ship because they were learning Langchain internals

Here's what DS looks like:
- 8 weeks to working infrastructure using battle-tested patterns
- Your team learns by pairing with us on live systems, not reading documentation
- You own the code, we own the knowledge transfer

Think of it this way: you're not paying us to build something you couldn't build. You're paying us to compress 12 months of trial-and-error into 8 weeks of working systems. Your platform team still owns it. They just get there faster.

What's your product roadmap lose if your platform team is heads-down on AI infra for the next year?"

---

### Objection 3: "Why not just use AWS/Azure native tools?"
**What they mean:** "Hyperscalers give us consulting credits and 'free' support."

**Response:**
"Hyperscaler native tools are great — when you're all-in on one cloud. But here's the trap:

**Lock-in architectures:** AWS Bedrock is fantastic until you want to use Anthropic's Claude, which requires different APIs. Now you're rewriting integrations.

**Conflicted incentives:** Their professional services team is measured on cloud consumption. Their job is to sell you more AWS, not optimize your costs.

**No cross-cloud expertise:** If you're multi-cloud (or plan to be), AWS consultants can't help with Azure, and vice versa.

**What DS does differently:**
- Vendor-agnostic architecture: We use managed services where they make sense, but you're not locked to one provider's roadmap
- Cost optimization: We have no cloud margin to protect, so we optimize for your costs, not vendor revenue
- Integration layer: We make Anthropic, OpenAI, Pinecone, Datadog, and Postgres work together — not just AWS services talking to AWS services

You're not choosing between DS and AWS. You're choosing between a multi-cloud integration layer (DS) and a single-vendor architecture (AWS). Which gives you more negotiating leverage in 2 years?

Want to see what a vendor-agnostic AI stack looks like?"

---

### Objection 4: "How is this different from DevOps consulting?"
**What they mean:** "We already tried this with our last DevOps consultancy. They sold us Kubernetes and left."

**Response:**
"Fair question. Most 'Platform Engineering' consultancies are DevOps shops with updated branding. They'll sell you CI/CD pipelines, Kubernetes, and Terraform — but that's not Platform Engineering.

Here's the difference:

**DevOps focuses on infrastructure automation:** Deploy faster, scale better, reduce downtime.

**Platform Engineering focuses on developer experience as product:** Your internal platform is a product your developers consume. Success = developers self-serve AI infrastructure without filing tickets.

**What that looks like in practice:**
- DevOps shop: 'We deployed your ML model to Kubernetes with auto-scaling.'
- DS: 'We built Golden Path templates so any engineer can deploy ML models with monitoring, cost attribution, and compliance audit trails pre-configured. Your platform team reviews pull requests instead of answering Slack messages.'

**Platform Engineering is about reducing cognitive load:**
- Your data scientists shouldn't learn Kubernetes
- Your platform team shouldn't be ticket-takers
- Your SREs shouldn't debug bespoke deployment pipelines

We build self-service AI infrastructure. DevOps shops build infrastructure. See the difference?

What does your platform team spend most of their time on — roadmap work or answering questions?"
