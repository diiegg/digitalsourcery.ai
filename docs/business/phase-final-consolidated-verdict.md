## THE PIVOTED BUSINESS MODEL

### What Changed From Original Proposal

| Original | Pivoted |
|----------|---------|
| Two standalone tiers (Foundation + Intelligence) | Intelligence IS the product. Foundation is embedded, never sold alone. |
| "We do platform engineering AND AI" | "We make your platform intelligent" |
| Horizontal (all industries) | Vertical (Fintech + Healthtech first) |
| Hourly/project pricing | Value-based: Assessment → Sprint → Retainer |
| Revenue = projects | Revenue = retainers (projects are CAC) |

### The Pivoted Offering Stack

```
DIGITAL SOURCERY — "We build the intelligence layer for your infrastructure"

ENTRY POINT (Lead Gen)
└── AI Readiness Assessment — $5-15K, 1-2 weeks
    "Where is your infrastructure intelligent, and where is it blind?"

CORE OFFERING (Revenue)
└── AI-Augmented Observability Sprint — $80-100K, 8 weeks
    "We make your platform predict problems before they page your team"
    (Includes necessary Foundation/Platform Eng work — embedded, not separate)

MOAT (Recurring Revenue)
└── Fractional CAIO Retainer — $8-10K/month
    "Ongoing AI infrastructure leadership without a $350K hire"
    (THIS is the business. Everything else is customer acquisition.)
```

### Why This Works

1. **Assessment → Sprint → Retainer** is a natural escalation
2. Each stage proves value before asking for more commitment
3. The retainer creates recurring revenue AND deepens the moat (you know their stack intimately)
4. Foundation work happens inside sprints — clients get it, but it's not what they're buying

## IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (Validated)

### Firmographics
- **Size:** 200-2000 employees
- **Revenue:** $20M-500M
- **Verticals:** Fintech, Healthtech (regulatory moats, real budgets)
- **Cloud:** AWS or GCP primary, some multi-cloud
- **Engineering team:** 20-200 engineers

### Behavioral Triggers (WHY they buy NOW)
1. **Failed incident** — downtime cost them money, board asking questions
2. **AI mandate from CEO/board** — "we need AI" but team can't deliver
3. **Scaling pain** — infrastructure can't handle growth
4. **Compliance pressure** — SOC2/HIPAA audit failures, EU AI Act
5. **Competitive threat** — competitor shipped AI features faster

### Disqualifiers (DO NOT pursue)
- < 100 employees (no budget)
- > 5000 employees (procurement hell, want Big 4)
- No cloud infrastructure (too far behind)
- "We just need a DevOps engineer" (wrong buyer, wrong need)
- Active RFP with Big 4 shortlisted (you'll lose on brand)

### The Buying Committee
| Role | Function | What They Care About |
|------|----------|---------------------|
| VP Engineering | Champion | Team velocity, reliability, developer experience |
| CTO | Economic Buyer | Strategic positioning, AI roadmap, board reporting |
| Director SRE/Platform | Influencer | Technical credibility, will this actually work? |
| CFO | Economic Influencer | ROI, cost reduction, budget justification |
