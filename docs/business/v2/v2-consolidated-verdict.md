## VALIDATED SERVICES (What to Sell)

### Tier 1: Lead With (Solo-Deliverable, Strongest Demand)

**Intelligent Observability / Autonomous Operations**
- What: AI anomaly detection, predictive incidents, self-healing, automated remediation
- Why first: Clearest pain point, proven ROI, every company with infrastructure needs this
- Price: $40-75K pilot, $10-20K/month managed service
- Delivery: 8-12 week pilot, solo founder can deliver

**AI-Driven Platform Engineering**
- What: LLM developer portals, natural language provisioning, automated golden paths
- Why: Strongest trend score (9.2/10), 80% adoption, $5-10M budgets
- Price: $50-80K projects
- Delivery: Solo-deliverable for single workload implementations

### Tier 2: Add After First Hire

**AI Platforms for Enterprises**
- What: GPU clusters, MLOps pipelines, model serving, agentic workflows
- Why defer: Heavy hyperscaler competition, requires deeper ML expertise
- Price: $75-150K projects
- Delivery: Needs second engineer (ML/AI specialist)

### Tier 3: Bundle (Never Standalone)

**AI FinOps**
- What: AI cost optimization — tokens, GPU utilization, inference cost reduction
- Why bundle: 73% of companies exceed AI budgets, 82% gross margin, natural extension
- Price: Bundled into other services or $5-10K add-on

**Legacy AI Modernization**
- What: Making old systems AI-capable without ripping them out
- Why bundle: $25-56B market, but too broad as standalone. Emerges naturally in engagements.

**Platform Foundations**
- What: The BlackMagickOps capabilities (platform eng, automation, observability)
- Why bundle: Commodity as standalone. Included in intelligence engagements as necessary base work.

## ICP (All Industries)

### Firmographics
- **Revenue:** $50M-500M
- **Employees:** 200-2000
- **Engineering team:** 30-200 engineers
- **Industries:** Manufacturing, energy, logistics, retail, SaaS, healthcare, finance — ALL
- **Cloud:** AWS or GCP primary, some multi-cloud
- **AI status:** Experimenting or early production (NOT "haven't started" and NOT "fully mature")

### Buyer Personas

**VP of Engineering (Manufacturing/Energy/Logistics)**
- Pain: Legacy systems, pressure to add AI capabilities, team doesn't have skills
- Trigger: Board/CEO AI mandate, competitor shipped AI features, production incident
- Budget: $50-100K approvable without extended procurement

**CTO (Vertical SaaS)**
- Pain: AI prototypes stuck in notebooks, expensive GPU bills, team overwhelmed
- Trigger: Failed AI pilot, cloud cost spike, customer demanding AI features
- Budget: $75-200K for strategic initiatives

**Director of AI/ML (Enterprise)**
- Pain: Models don't make it to production, no MLOps, governance gaps
- Trigger: Audit failure, model drift incident, scaling bottleneck
- Budget: $50-150K within existing AI budget

### Disqualifiers
- < 100 employees (no budget)
- > 5000 employees (procurement hell)
- No cloud infrastructure (too far behind)
- "We just need bodies" (staff aug, not strategy)
- Active Big 4 engagement (won't switch mid-project)

2. **"AI That Pays for Itself"** — 73% of companies overspend on AI. We optimize tokens, GPU utilization, and inference costs so your AI investment has positive ROI.

3. **"Engineers Who Ship, Not Consultants Who Present"** — Senior engineers writing production code alongside your team. No PowerPoints. No "transformation frameworks."

4. **"Your Systems, Made Intelligent"** — Legacy systems aren't dead — they need an intelligence layer. We build on top of what works, not replace what's running.

5. **"Full-Stack AI Operations"** — From anomaly detection to self-healing clusters to model serving. One team, one engagement, one codebase you own.
